# As-Is and To-Be

## As Is

- Understand the current state of the system
- Identify strengths and weaknesses
- Pinpoint challenges and areas where improvements could be made

## Steps of As Is Analysis

### Research

- Get list of products and services and then identify the processes that the company 
uses to generate those products and services

### Personal interviews

Interview stakeholders who perform each process as well as the managers or other
subject matter experts involved in the process. Interview multiple people to 
ensure a more complete picture of the process. Be careful not to make conclusions
based solely on any one person's report.

###  Direct Observation

'nuff said.

### Surveys 

Send surveys or questionnaires to process participants to collect formal written
responses. Surveys allow you to ask specific questions that may not comes from
observation or interviews

### Group meetings

Group meetings with relevant stakeholders.

### Document

Document using a Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN Diagram)

### Bottlenecks, Gaps and Weaknesses

- Bottlenecks are where your processes are stalled
- Gaps are places that aren't in your process that hinder your desired performance/ outcome
- Weaknesses are existing processes that could be better

## To Be

- Proposing changes or improvements based on your 'as-is' analysis
- Designing solutions or suggesting new processes, tools, or
technologies that can enhance the system
- Thinking strategically about how to address the identified 
weaknesses and leverage opportunities for innovation

### What is the why?

What is the desired outcome for the project?
