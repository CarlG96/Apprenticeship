# Probability

No probability can be outside the range 0 to 1.

## Complementary Events

Two events are complementary if one of them must happen and, when it does,
the other event cannot.

The sum of two complementary events must always equal 1. This is known
as total probability.

## Calculating Theoretical Probabilities

P of obtaining our chosen event = number of ways the chosen event can occur/ total number of possibilities

eg chance of rolling 5 on a dice is 1/6

## Calculating Experimental Probabilities

If you don't have sufficient information to calculate a theoretical probability then you can
use experimental probability. Here is the formula:

Probability that chosen event occurs = number of ways the chosen event occurs / total number of times the experiment is repeated

## Independent Events

Two events are independent if the occurrence of either one in no way affects the occurrence of the other.

If events A and B are independent, then the probability of obtaining A and B is given by:

P(A and B) = P(A) X P(B)
