# Network Media

Network media refers to the various types of physical and wireless connections used to link computers and other devices in a network. These media are essential for transmitting data, voice and video across networks, enabling communication and resource sharing among devices. The choice of network can significantly impact the performance, reliability, and security of a network.

## Cables

Network cables are the physical medium through which data is transmitted between devices in a network.

## Twisted-pair cables

Twisted-pair cables are a type of cable used to connect devices within a LAN. They are essential for wired networking, providing a reliable and high-speed connection for data transfer between computers, routers, switches, and other network devices. Ethernet cables are widely used in both residential and commercial settings due to their efficiency and ease of use.

## Fiber Optic Cables

Fiber optic cables are a type of network cable that uses light to transmit data. Unlike traditional copper cables, which use electrical signals, fiber optic cables use light pulses to carry information. This allows for much higher data transfer rates and longer transmission distances without signal degradation. Fiber optic technology is essential for modern high-speed internet, telecommunications, and data networks.

| Fibre Type             | Core Diameter       | Usage                                              | Advantages                                      | Disadvantages                                      |
|------------------------|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Single-Mode Fibre (SMF)| 8-10 micrometers    | Long-distance communication, such as in telecommunications and internet backbones | High bandwidth, low signal loss over long distances | More expensive, requires precise alignment of light sources |
| Multi-Mode Fibre (MMF) | 50-62.5 micrometers | Shorter distances, such as within buildings or campus networks | Easier to install and align, generally less expensive than SMF | Higher signal loss, lower bandwidth over long distances |

## Cisco Serial

A cisco serial connection refers to the use of serial interfaces on Cisco networking devices, such as routers and switches, to connect to other network devices or to establish WAN links.

Serial connections are still used, though their prevalence has decreased with the advent of faster and more efficient networking technologies. You may find them in legacy systems and for redundancy.


## Wifi 

Wi-Fi is a technology that enables devices to connect to the internet and communicate with eachother wirelessly using radio waves. Provides seamless connectivity for a wide range of devices, such as smartphones, laptops etc.
