# Being Pragmatic

## Be pragmatic and go only as far as you must

It's easy to get stuck in an infinite loop of design improvements, especially for engineers
who appreciate a well-designed system. Even simple design decisions can branch out into numerous
possibilities.

## Refactor aggressively but in small steps

Never stop refactoring. Refactoring is the most effective tool to combat the growth of complexity.
The more frequently you refactor, the cheaper and quicker it becomes because you have less code 
to refactor, and your skills will improve.

## Accept that your code won't ever be perfect

Life as an engineer becomes much easier when you realise that your code, design and architecture
will never be perfect.
